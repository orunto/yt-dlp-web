# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be respectful. Contribute in good faith. Do not make this space unpleasant for others.

## Expected behaviour

- Communicate clearly and directly. Assume good intent.
- Give useful feedback when reviewing code. Explain your reasoning.
- Accept feedback on your own work without taking it personally. Everyone improves through critique.
- Be patient with newcomers. Everyone starts somewhere.
- Stay on topic. This project exists to build software, not to debate unrelated matters.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language of any kind.
- Dismissing or belittling contributions without explanation.
- Deliberate misinformation about the project, its contributors, or how it works.
- Submitting malicious code, regardless of how it is framed.

## On the use of AI tools

AI assistance is permitted but must be used responsibly:

- Do not submit AI-generated code that you have not read, tested, and taken responsibility for.
- Do not use AI tools to generate large quantities of low-quality issues, pull requests, or comments.
- Do not misrepresent AI-generated work as original human authorship when directly asked.

The standard for AI-assisted contributions is the same as for any other contribution: it must be correct, safe, and reviewed before it reaches this project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove content or restrict access for behaviour that violates this document. Decisions will be explained.

## Reporting

If you witness or experience behaviour that violates this code of conduct, contact the maintainers directly through GitHub. Reports will be handled discreetly.
